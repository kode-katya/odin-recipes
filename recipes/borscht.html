Borscht

Russian borscht is a traditional beet-based soup that has become a symbol of Eastern European cuisine, particularly in Russia and Ukraine. This hearty soup is renowned for its vibrant deep-red color, which comes from the beets, the main ingredient. Borscht typically includes a variety of vegetables such as cabbage, potatoes, carrots, and onions, along with beets. It can be made with or without meat, with beef or pork often used for a richer flavor. The soup is slow-cooked, allowing the flavors to meld together, resulting in a deeply savory dish with a slight sweetness from the beets.

Borscht is often served hot, topped with a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkling of fresh dill, which adds a cool, tangy contrast to the warm, earthy flavors of the soup.
