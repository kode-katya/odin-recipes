Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyaki is a popular Japanese savory pancake that originated in Osaka and has become a beloved dish across Japan. The name "okonomiyaki" roughly translates to "grill what you like," reflecting the dish's versatility and customizability. Its basic ingredients include a batter made from flour, eggs, shredded cabbage, and water or dashi (Japanese soup stock). Additional ingredients can vary widely, often including pork, seafood, or vegetables. The pancake is cooked on a hot griddle and topped with okonomiyaki sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and sometimes seaweed. It's a flavorful and hearty dish enjoyed in homes and specialized restaurants alike.

In addition to its delicious flavor, okonomiyaki is also known for its interactive dining experience. Many restaurants allow diners to cook their own okonomiyaki at the table, providing a communal and fun atmosphere. The batter is poured onto a hot iron griddle, where customers can add their preferred toppings and watch it sizzle to perfection. Different regions in Japan have their own variations of the dish, with Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki being one of the most famous. Unlike the Osaka version, Hiroshima-style layers the ingredients, often including noodles like yakisoba or udon. This regional diversity and the dish's adaptability make okonomiyaki a true representation of Japanese comfort food culture.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of shredded cabbage
  • 11⁄4 cups of scallions
  • 1 cup of panko breadcrumbs
  • 3⁄4 teaspoon of sea salt
  • 3 large, beaten eggs
  • Extra-virgin olive oil

Optional Ingredients

  • Mayonnaise
  • Sesame seeds
  • 1⁄2 sheet of nori
  • 1⁄2 cup of microgreens

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine the cabbage, scallions, panko, and salt.
  2. Gently mix the eggs.
  3. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat.
  4. Brush the skillet with olive oil and use a 1⁄4 cup of batter and pour it into the skillet.
  5. Flatten the top using a spatula and let it cook for 3 minutes per side, or until golden brown.
  6. Repeat steps 4-5 until no batter remains.
  7. Top the okonomiyaki with some of the optional ingredients & enjoy.
